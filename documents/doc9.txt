Cosine similarity is commonly used in ranked retrieval because it compares the direction of two vectors rather than only their size. In a search engine, the query can be represented as a vector and each document can also be represented as a vector. If the query vector and document vector point in a similar direction, the document receives a higher similarity score. This is helpful because longer documents should not automatically win just because they contain more words. Cosine similarity gives a balanced way to compare documents with different lengths, especially when combined with TF-IDF weighting.
