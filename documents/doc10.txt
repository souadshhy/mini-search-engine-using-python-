Document preprocessing prepares raw text before indexing. The usual steps include converting text to lowercase, removing punctuation, splitting the text into tokens, removing stop words, and applying stemming or lemmatization. These steps reduce noise and help different forms of a word match each other. For example, learning, learned, and learns may be reduced to a similar root depending on the chosen method. Preprocessing is not only a technical step; it also affects search quality. If the preprocessing is too aggressive, meaningful words may be damaged. If it is too weak, the index may become noisy.
