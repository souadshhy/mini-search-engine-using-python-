Data mining focuses on discovering useful patterns inside large collections of data. A company might use data mining to understand customer behavior, a hospital might study treatment records, and a university might analyze student performance trends. The goal is not just to store data, but to turn it into meaningful information. Data mining often uses statistics, machine learning, and database techniques together. Before any discovery happens, the raw data usually needs cleaning because real datasets contain missing values, repeated records, and inconsistent formatting. Without preprocessing, even a strong algorithm can produce weak or misleading results.
