TF-IDF is a weighting method used to estimate how important a term is in a document collection. Term frequency measures how often a word appears in a document. Inverse document frequency reduces the importance of words that appear in many documents. The basic idea is that a word repeated in one document may be useful, but a word repeated everywhere is probably less helpful for distinguishing documents. In a ranked retrieval system, TF-IDF values can turn documents and queries into vectors. After that, the search engine can compare them mathematically and return documents in a useful order.
