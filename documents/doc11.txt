Stemming is a simple normalization technique that cuts words down to a root-like form. It is fast and useful for small information retrieval projects, but the output is not always a real word. For example, a stemmer may shorten computing and computer in a way that looks strange to a reader. Lemmatization is more language-aware because it tries to return the dictionary form of a word. It usually gives cleaner terms, but it can require more resources. Comparing stemming and lemmatization is a good experiment because the choice can change the vocabulary size and retrieval results.
