A database management system helps users store, organize, and retrieve structured data. Tables, records, keys, and queries are common parts of database work. In many software systems, the database is the reliable place where important information is kept. Search engines are different from database systems because they usually work with unstructured text and uncertain relevance. Still, the two areas connect. A search engine may store document metadata in a database, while a database application may need text search features for descriptions, names, or logs.
